Ability / Hazards / Safety

This project is not a beginner DIY project, and requires a large amount of attention to details as well as the correct tools to complete the job. Do not begin this project if you do not believe you can complete it alone, or if you are not confident dissasembling the shell of a onewheeled device.

Resource Info

Just as there are many pros to converting your board into a VESC, or even building one from scratch, there are many cons as well.
Some of the pros include right to repair, extra torque, off road maneuverability, runs cooler, customizable, consistent updates and work being done from the community contributors, etc... While some of the cons include difficulty to assemble, very advanced settings that are not easy for beginners, unfamiliarity with controls, and major risk of injury due to lack of safety features.

We have prepared information about and places to buy motor controllers, hub motors, tires, Bluetooth modules, motherboard protector, air pumps and other parts & tools for your own DIY VESC board. By the end of your build, you will fully understand how a VESC works and what it is capable of.
